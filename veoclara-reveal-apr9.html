Why make a fake product page?

Because polished gadgets get attention faster than dirty streets do.

This page borrows the language of a premium product launch for one reason: people stop and look. The reveal comes quickly, because the point is not to fool people for long. It is to redirect that attention toward a problem that should already feel unacceptable.

The fake part is the product. The images, launch cues, and product polish are there to win the first glance.
The real part is the ask. Cleanliness and hygienic living conditions should be treated like real infrastructure, not background inconvenience.
The action is simple. Sign the statement, add your state, and make the demand visible.

FAQ / The Honest Part

After the product fantasy wears off, the page should still feel useful.

These are the questions a real launch page would hide and an awareness campaign should answer plainly.

Is VeoClara a real product? No. It is a satirical concept built to borrow the language of tech launches and redirect that attention toward public cleanliness.
Why make it look premium first? Because polished products get attention immediately. The campaign uses that reflex, then turns it into a more uncomfortable question about what we ignore in real life.
What happens if I sign the statement? In this prototype, the page counter updates locally to show the campaign mechanic. Before public launch, this needs a real form backend and a clear privacy note.
Are the tallies and map already live campaign data? No. They are placeholder visuals for the prototype. A public version should replace them with real submissions, source links, and transparent methodology.
Why not just build a real app instead? The problem is not a missing interface. It is missing urgency, accountability, and maintenance.
